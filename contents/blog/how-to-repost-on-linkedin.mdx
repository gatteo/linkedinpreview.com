Reposting on LinkedIn takes 10 seconds. Done right, it keeps you visible, amplifies great content, and occasionally gets you reach you didn't have to build from scratch.

Done wrong (or done constantly without adding value), it trains your audience to scroll past you.

Research analyzing 1.8 million LinkedIn posts revealed something counterintuitive: **instant reposts (no commentary) outperform "repost with thoughts" algorithmically by 12x**. But that doesn't mean you should never add commentary.

In this guide, I'll show you exactly how to repost on LinkedIn, when to use each option, what content is worth sharing, and the common mistakes that kill reach.

## How to Repost on LinkedIn (Step-by-Step)

The process is identical on desktop and mobile:

**Step 1:** Find a post you want to share in your feed (or visit the profile of someone whose content you want to amplify).

**Step 2:** Click the share icon below the post (it looks like a curved arrow).

**Step 3:** Choose one of two options:
- **Repost** (instant share, no commentary)
- **Repost with your thoughts** (add your own commentary above the original post)

**Step 4:** If you chose "Repost with your thoughts," write your commentary in the text box that appears.

**Step 5:** Click **Post**.

That's it. The original post (with your commentary if you added it) appears in your feed and is distributed to your network.

## Repost vs. Repost With Thoughts: Which Should You Use?

This is where most people get it wrong - and where the data is genuinely surprising.

### Instant Repost (No Commentary)

Research based on LinkedIn's Algorithm Insights Report (analyzing 1.8M posts) found that **instant reposts outperform "repost with thoughts" algorithmically**.

Here's what the data shows:

**From the original poster's perspective:**
- **Instant repost (within 4 hours):** Boosts the original post's growth by **+40%**
- **Repost with thoughts:** Reduces the original post's impact by **×12** compared to instant repost

The reason? An instant repost gives algorithmic credit to the original post. LinkedIn reads it as a genuine endorsement rather than new content competing for attention.

**From your perspective (the reposter):**
- **Limited personal reach:** An instant repost boosts the original post by 4% but doesn't meaningfully increase YOUR visibility
- **It's a gesture, not a growth tactic:** Use instant repost to support someone, not to grow your own following

**When to use instant repost:**
- The original post is strong enough to stand alone
- You want to amplify a colleague, client, or industry voice
- You don't have a unique perspective to add
- You're showing up consistently without creating from scratch

### Repost With Your Thoughts

Adding commentary turns the repost into NEW content under your name. It positions you as someone with a perspective, not just a signal booster.

But here's the catch: **without fast engagement, your repost with thoughts reaches only 8-10% of your audience.** Hit 10+ engagements and that climbs to 15-20%, but you may need to actively prompt your network.

**When to use repost with thoughts:**
- You have a genuine perspective to add (not just "Great post!")
- You disagree with part of the original and can add a reasoned counterpoint
- The post gives you a jumping-off point for a broader insight
- You want to contextualize the content for your specific audience
- You're tagging someone and explaining why you're sharing their work

**Critical rule:** Your commentary should be **at least 100 words or highly personal**. Shorter commentary underperforms by **×3**. If you can't write 100+ words of actual value, use instant repost instead.

## 5 Frameworks for Adding Value to Reposts

Don't add commentary just to add it. A repost that says "Great post, worth reading!" adds zero value and signals you're filling space.

Use these five frameworks when you genuinely have something to contribute:

### 1. The "Key Takeaway" Framework

Summarize the single most important insight for YOUR audience. Tell them why it matters to them specifically.

**Example:**
"The most important line in this post: 'Saves now carry more algorithmic weight than likes.' This changes everything about how we should be creating LinkedIn content in 2026. Stop chasing vanity metrics. Start creating save-worthy resources."

### 2. The "Provocative Question" Framework

Use the original post as a jumping-off point to ask your network a thoughtful question. This sparks comments and discussion.

**Example:**
"This data shows that daily posting doesn't increase reach. But I'm curious - for those of you posting 5-7x/week, are you seeing diminishing returns? Or does your industry reward higher frequency?"

### 3. The "Personal Anecdote" Framework

Connect the post to a relevant experience. Show how the insight applies to your own work.

**Example:**
"We tested this exact strategy last quarter. Switched from daily posts to 3x/week with higher quality. Result: 2x more profile visits, 40% higher engagement rate. The frequency myth is real."

### 4. The "Data-Driven" Framework

Add a statistic or data point that supports, complements, or challenges the original post.

**Example:**
"Adding to this: our analysis of 500 LinkedIn newsletters found that consistent weekly publishing drives 3x more subscriber growth than monthly. The key word is 'consistent' - sporadic publishing kills momentum."

### 5. The "Actionable Tip" Framework

Distill the information into a concrete, actionable tip your audience can implement immediately.

**Example:**
"Here's how to apply this today: Block 60 minutes this week to batch-write 3 LinkedIn posts. Use this framework for each one: hook (2 lines) → insight (3-5 bullets) → CTA (question). Preview them before scheduling."

## What Content Should You Repost?

Not every post deserves a share. The best reposts fall into these categories:

### Industry News and Research

Data, reports, and breaking developments in your field. Reposting these positions you as someone who stays current.

When to instant repost: The news speaks for itself.
When to add commentary: You have insider context or a unique angle on the implications.

### User-Generated Content (UGC) and Customer Wins

Did a customer post about success using your product? Repost it immediately. This is powerful social proof that builds trust more than any branded ad.

**Always add commentary** to thank them and provide context about the result.

### Employee Advocacy

When an employee shares a company win or thought leadership piece, repost from the company page (if you manage it). This celebrates your team and amplifies their voice.

**Note:** Employee reposts of company content have reduced impact (-30% reach) due to LinkedIn's pod detection. Use strategically, not systematically.

### Partner and Influencer Content

Amplify content from official partners or influencers who align with your brand values. This strengthens relationships and exposes you to their audiences.

### Thought Leadership from Industry Experts

Sharing insightful posts from recognized experts shows you're engaged in broader industry conversations and respect intellectual contributions from others.

**When to instant repost:** The expert's name carries the credibility.
**When to add commentary:** You can add practical application or connect it to your own experience.

## What to Avoid Reposting

### 1. Reposting Constantly Without Original Content

If your feed is 80% reposts, your audience tunes you out. Reposts should complement original posts, not replace them.

**Good rule:** The 4-1-1 ratio. For every 6 posts:
- 4 should be curated/reposted content
- 1 should be original thought leadership
- 1 can be a soft promotional post

### 2. Coordinated Team Reposts

When employees simultaneously repost company content, LinkedIn's algorithm detects pod behavior. Reach impact drops by ~30% and distribution credit goes to the original post, not the sharers.

### 3. Reposting Content You Haven't Read

Amplifying something you haven't fully read is a reputational risk. Skim shares occasionally backfire badly, especially if the content contains controversial takes or outdated information.

### 4. Adding Generic Commentary

"Great post!" or "Worth reading!" adds nothing. If you can't write 100+ words of actual value, use instant repost instead.

### 5. Reposting Your Own Underperforming Posts

Reposting your own content 24-48 hours after publishing is legitimate (see below), but only for posts that performed well the first time. Don't repost content that flopped - analyze why it didn't work instead.

## Should You Repost Your Own LinkedIn Posts?

Yes - reposting your own high-performing content 24-48 hours after the original publish is a legitimate reach extension strategy.

LinkedIn's algorithm now keeps strong posts circulating for 2-3 weeks. A well-timed repost catches a second wave of engagement from:
- Connections in different time zones who missed it the first time
- People who saw it initially but didn't engage
- New followers who weren't in your network when you first posted

**How to do it right:**
1. **Wait until engagement slows:** Let the initial wave run its course (usually 24-48 hours)
2. **Add new context:** Don't just repost - add commentary about what you learned from the comments or how your thinking evolved
3. **Only repost winners:** If a post underperformed, fix the approach rather than resharing it

**Example repost commentary:**
"Reposting this from yesterday because the comments raised something I hadn't considered: [insight from comments]. Here's how that changes my original take: [new perspective]."

## Why You Can't Repost a LinkedIn Post

If the repost button is missing or greyed out, one of these is true:

### The Post Is Set to Private

The author restricted visibility to connections only. You can't repost private posts.

### The Post Is from a LinkedIn Group

Group content can't be reposted outside the group. This protects group confidentiality.

### The Author Disabled Resharing

LinkedIn allows creators to turn off reposting on individual posts. If disabled, there's nothing you can do except screenshot and share manually (with proper credit).

## Should You Repost as a Company Page?

If you manage a LinkedIn company page, you can repost content as the page rather than your personal profile.

The process is the same (click share, choose repost type), but you'll be prompted to select whether you're posting as yourself or as the page.

**Important caveat:** Organic company page posts get very limited feed visibility in 2026 (around 2% of total feed exposure without employee amplification). Company pages work better for paid promotion than organic reach.

**Best use:** Repost strong content from employees' personal profiles onto the company page to keep it active without expecting much organic distribution.

## Common Mistakes That Kill Repost Performance

### Mistake #1: Reposting Everything You See

Quality > quantity. Your network judges you by what you amplify. Be selective. Only share content that genuinely adds value for your audience.

### Mistake #2: Not Vetting Sources

Sharing misinformation (even unintentionally) damages your credibility. Check the source before you repost.

### Mistake #3: Ignoring Timeliness

Check the date before sharing articles or data. The digital world moves fast - outdated content makes you look out of touch.

### Mistake #4: Reposting and Ghosting

If people comment on your repost, reply to them. Engagement tells the algorithm your content is valuable - and it tells your community you're listening.

### Mistake #5: Repeating What the Original Said

If you're adding commentary, don't just summarize the original post. Your audience can read it themselves. Add a NEW layer of insight.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between repost and repost with thoughts?

**Repost** (instant share, no commentary) boosts the original post's reach by 40% and gives algorithmic credit to the original author. **Repost with thoughts** creates new content under your name but reaches only 8-10% of your audience unless it gets fast engagement. Use instant repost to amplify, use commentary when you have genuine value to add.

### Should I always add commentary when reposting?

No - counterintuitively, instant reposts outperform commentary by 12x algorithmically. Only add commentary when you have at least 100 words of genuine insight. If you can't add meaningful value, use instant repost instead.

### Can I repost my own LinkedIn posts?

Yes. Reposting your own high-performing content 24-48 hours after publishing is legitimate. Wait until initial engagement slows, add new context about what you learned, and only repost content that performed well the first time.

### Why can't I repost a LinkedIn post?

Three reasons: (1) the post is set to private (connections only), (2) the post is from a LinkedIn group (group content can't be reposted), or (3) the author disabled resharing on that specific post.

### How many times should I repost per week?

Follow the 4-1-1 rule: for every 6 posts, 4 should be reposts/curated content, 1 should be original thought leadership, and 1 can be promotional. Don't let reposts replace original content - they should complement it.

### Does reposting help the LinkedIn algorithm?

Instant reposting (no commentary) boosts the ORIGINAL post's reach by 4% but doesn't meaningfully increase your own visibility. Reposting with thoughts creates new content that can drive engagement if you add substantial value (100+ words of genuine insight).

Reposting on LinkedIn is a double-edged sword. Done strategically, it keeps you visible, amplifies great content, and fills gaps in your content calendar. Done thoughtlessly, it trains your audience to scroll past you.

- **Use instant repost** to amplify others without adding commentary that doesn't add value
- **Use repost with thoughts** when you have 100+ words of genuine insight to contribute
- **Repost your own high-performing content** 24-48 hours later to extend reach
