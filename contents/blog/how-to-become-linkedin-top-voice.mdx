A LinkedIn Top Voice badge is one of the most coveted status symbols on the platform. It instantly signals authority, builds trust with your profile visitors, and helps you stand out in a crowded feed.

But in recent years, LinkedIn has introduced different tiers of the Top Voice program. If you are wondering how to get the badge in 2026, you first need to understand which badge you are actually aiming for.

There are currently two distinct Top Voice badges: the **Blue Badge** (invite-only) and the **Gold Badge** (community-earned). 

In this guide, we will break down the differences and show you the exact steps to earn both.

## 1. The Blue Badge: LinkedIn Top Voices (Invite-Only)

The blue Top Voice badge is the original and most prestigious award. It is given directly by the LinkedIn editorial team to senior experts and creators who consistently publish high-impact, original content.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Color:** Blue icon next to your name.
- **Exclusivity:** Invite-only. You cannot apply for it.
- **Focus:** Awarded for your overall presence, original posts, articles, and thought leadership.
- **Permanence:** It stays on your profile indefinitely (unless revoked for community violations).

### How to Position Yourself for the Blue Badge

Because it's invite-only, there is no exact formula. However, the creators who earn it share several common traits:

1. **Own a Specific Niche:** Don't talk about everything. Pick one clear area of expertise (e.g., "B2B SaaS Marketing" or "AI in Healthcare") and stick to it.
2. **Publish Consistently:** You need a track record of high-quality posts and LinkedIn Articles over several months or years.
3. **Drive Meaningful Conversations:** LinkedIn looks for creators whose posts generate thoughtful comments, not just superficial likes. Ask questions, reply to comments, and foster debate.
4. **Avoid Engagement Bait:** LinkedIn's editorial team actively avoids rewarding accounts that use engagement pods or low-effort viral hooks.

## 2. The Gold Badge: Community Top Voices (Earned)

The gold badge was introduced to recognize members who contribute valuable insights to LinkedIn's **Collaborative Articles** (AI-generated articles that prompt users to add their perspective).

**Key characteristics:**
- **Color:** Gold icon next to your name, usually tied to a specific skill (e.g., "Top Sales Voice").
- **Exclusivity:** Open to anyone who contributes enough high-quality insights.
- **Focus:** Awarded strictly based on your contributions to collaborative articles.
- **Permanence:** Temporary. It lasts for 60 days. You must keep contributing to maintain it.

### How to Earn the Gold Community Badge

Earning the gold badge requires a systematic approach to collaborative articles. Here is how to get it:

**Step 1: Find Relevant Articles**
Search LinkedIn for your core skill (e.g., "SEO" or "Leadership"). Look for the "Collaborative Articles" filter. 

**Step 2: Add Original Insights**
When you find an article, you will see prompts asking for your perspective. Do not just summarize what the AI already wrote. Add personal anecdotes, specific frameworks, or real-world examples. 

**Step 3: Earn "Insightful" Reactions**
The badge isn't just about volume; it's about quality. Other users can react to your contribution with the "Insightful" lightbulb. LinkedIn's algorithm uses these reactions to determine who the top 5% of contributors are.

**Step 4: Be Consistent**
Aim to contribute 3-5 high-quality insights per week in the same skill category. If you reach the top 5% of contributors for that skill, you will be awarded the badge.

## Which Badge Should You Focus On?

While the **Blue Badge** carries significantly more prestige and industry weight, it is entirely out of your direct control. It should be viewed as a long-term byproduct of building a great personal brand.

The **Gold Badge**, on the other hand, is a gamified system that you can actively work towards today. It is a great starting point if you want to build authority in a specific niche and increase your profile's visibility in search results.

However, beware of the trap of low-effort contributions. LinkedIn has been cracking down on users who use AI to generate their collaborative article responses. If you want the gold badge, make sure your contributions are genuinely yours.

## Summary

Earning a LinkedIn Top Voice badge in 2026 comes down to understanding the two paths:
- **Blue Badge:** Build a long-term, high-quality content engine on your personal feed.
- **Gold Badge:** Consistently add unique, human experiences to Collaborative Articles in your niche.

Regardless of which badge you are aiming for, the foundation is the same: writing clear, engaging, and well-formatted content.
