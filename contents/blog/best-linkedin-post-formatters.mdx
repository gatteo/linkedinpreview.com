## What is a LinkedIn Post Formatter?

A LinkedIn post formatter is a tool that lets you add text formatting to your LinkedIn posts that LinkedIn's native editor doesn't support. This includes bold text, italics, underline, strikethrough, bullet points, and numbered lists.

These formatters work by converting your text into Unicode characters that LinkedIn accepts. When you paste the formatted text into LinkedIn, it displays with all the formatting intact.

**Key features to look for in a post formatter:**

- **Bold, italic, and underline support** - basic text emphasis
- **Bullet points and numbered lists** - better structure and readability
- **Real-time preview** - see exactly how your post will look before publishing
- **Mobile preview** - LinkedIn is primarily mobile, so this is critical
- **No signup required** - saves time and protects privacy
- **Free access** - you shouldn't have to pay for basic formatting

## LinkedIn Formatting Best Practices

Now that you have a formatter, follow these guidelines to use formatting effectively:

**Don't overformat.** Formatting should emphasize key points, not decorate every sentence. If everything is bold, nothing stands out.

**Use bold for emphasis.** Reserve bold text for the most important phrases, key takeaways, or calls to action. Think 1-3 bold phrases per post, not 10.

**Lists improve scannability.** Bullet points and numbered lists break up text blocks and make posts easier to scan. Use them for steps, tips, or examples.

**Test on mobile first.** Most people read LinkedIn on their phones. Always check the mobile preview before publishing to make sure your formatting looks good on small screens.

**Keep paragraphs short.** Even with formatting, long paragraphs are hard to read. Aim for 2-4 sentences per paragraph.
