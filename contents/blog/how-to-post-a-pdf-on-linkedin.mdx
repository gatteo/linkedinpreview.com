Have you noticed those highly engaging, swipeable "carousel" posts dominating your LinkedIn feed? The secret behind them is surprisingly simple: **they are just PDF documents.**

When you upload a multi-page PDF to LinkedIn, the platform automatically converts it into a seamless, interactive carousel. It is one of the most effective ways to share visual guides, portfolios, and step-by-step tutorials with your professional network.

Whether you are sharing a whitepaper, a presentation, or a custom-designed carousel, here is everything you need to know about how to post a PDF on LinkedIn.

## How to Post a PDF on LinkedIn (Desktop)

Sharing a document on LinkedIn is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to upload your PDF from your computer:

1. Go to your LinkedIn feed and click **"Start a post"** at the top of the page.
2. In the post creation box, click the **"More" menu** (three dots) if you don't see all the icons, and select **"Add a document"**.
3. Click **"Choose file"** to browse your computer, or select a file directly from Dropbox or Google Drive.
4. Select your PDF file and wait for it to upload.
5. Add a **"Document title"** (this is important, as it will be visible to your readers above the document).
6. Click **"Done"**.
7. Write your accompanying post text in the main box to give context to your PDF.
8. Hit **"Publish"**.

## How to Post a PDF on LinkedIn (Mobile App)

You can also post PDFs directly from the LinkedIn mobile app (iOS and Android):

1. Open the LinkedIn app and tap **"Post"** (the plus icon at the bottom of the screen).
2. Tap the **three dots** to open the extended menu.
3. Select **"Document"**.
4. Choose the PDF file from your phone's files or connected cloud storage.
5. Add a descriptive title for your document.
6. Write your post caption and tap **"Post"**.

_Note: While you can upload from mobile, many creators prefer posting PDFs from desktop to better control the formatting of the accompanying text and ensure the document renders correctly._

## Why You Should Be Posting PDFs on LinkedIn

Uploading PDFs (carousels) is one of the highest-performing content formats on LinkedIn right now. Here is why you should incorporate them into your strategy:

- **Higher Engagement Rates:** Because users have to actively click or swipe through the pages, it signals high engagement to the LinkedIn algorithm, often resulting in broader reach.
- **Increased Dwell Time:** The longer someone spends swiping through your document, the more LinkedIn favors your post.
- **Visual Storytelling:** PDFs allow you to combine text, graphics, and branding in a way that standard text posts or single images cannot match.
- **High Retention:** Complex topics are easier to digest when broken down into visual slides.

## Best Practices for LinkedIn PDF Carousels

To ensure your document looks professional and gets maximum engagement, keep these technical specifications and best practices in mind:

### 1. Optimal Dimensions

While you can upload standard A4 or US Letter documents, the best dimensions for LinkedIn carousels are:

- **Square (1:1 aspect ratio):** 1080 x 1080 pixels. This looks great on both desktop and mobile.
- **Portrait (4:5 aspect ratio):** 1080 x 1350 pixels. This takes up more screen real estate on mobile devices, capturing more attention.

### 2. Keep the File Size Manageable

LinkedIn has strict limits for document uploads. Your PDF must be:

- Under **100 MB** in size.
- Under **300 pages** in length.
  (However, for maximum engagement, aim for 5 to 15 pages).

### 3. Design a Strong First Page

Your first slide acts as the thumbnail for the entire document. Treat it like a YouTube thumbnail: use large, bold text, high contrast, and a clear promise of what the reader will learn by swiping.

### 4. Include a Call-To-Action (CTA)

Always dedicate your final slide to a clear call-to-action. Tell your readers exactly what to do next, whether that is following your profile, leaving a comment, or visiting your website.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I track who downloads my PDF?

No. While LinkedIn allows users to download your document by clicking the download icon in the top right corner, you cannot see exactly who downloaded it. You can only view standard metrics like impressions, clicks, and engagement.

### Can I include clickable links in my PDF?

Yes, but with limitations. If you add hyperlinks to your PDF before exporting it, those links are usually clickable when users view the document on a desktop computer. However, hyperlinks inside PDFs do not consistently work on the LinkedIn mobile app. Always include your most important links in the post caption or the comments.

### Does LinkedIn compress my PDF?

Yes, LinkedIn compresses document files to optimize loading times. To prevent your text and images from looking blurry, always export your PDF in the highest possible quality (such as "Best for Web" or 300 DPI) before uploading.

### Can I edit a PDF after posting it?

No. While you can [edit the text of a LinkedIn post](/blog/how-to-edit-linkedin-post), you cannot replace or modify the attached PDF document. If you spot a mistake in your slides, you will need to delete the entire post and re-upload a corrected version.

## Summary

Posting a PDF on LinkedIn is the secret to creating those highly engaging, swipeable carousels you see in your feed. By designing your document with the right dimensions (1080x1080 or 1080x1350) and keeping your slides visually appealing, you can significantly increase your dwell time and reach on the platform. Remember to craft a compelling text hook to accompany your document, and always preview your formatting before hitting publish.
