Writing a LinkedIn layoff post is one of the most stressful things you can publish. You are processing a difficult change, but you also need to project professionalism to future employers.

A well-crafted layoff announcement can turn a bad situation into a massive influx of recruiter messages. The key is striking the right balance between authenticity, gratitude, and a clear call to action.

## Why You Should Write a LinkedIn Layoff Post

When you lose your job, your first instinct might be to stay quiet until you find a new one. But the modern job market moves through networks, and staying silent is a missed opportunity.

Announcing your layoff on LinkedIn serves three critical purposes:

- **It activates your network:** People cannot help you if they do not know you need it.
- **It controls the narrative:** You get to define your departure gracefully before rumors start.
- **It signals recruiters:** Hiring managers actively monitor the "#OpenToWork" feed for top talent recently released into the market.

Before you start typing, remember that this post is a marketing asset. It is not a diary entry. You are selling your resilience and your skills.

## The 4-Part Layoff Post Framework

The most successful [LinkedIn post formats](/blog/linkedin-post-format) follow a predictable structure. For a layoff announcement, use this four-part framework.

### 1. The Direct Opening

Do not bury the lead. Start by stating clearly what happened. Keep it professional and objective.

- **Bad:** "I cannot believe the corporate greed that just ruined my life..."
- **Good:** "Unfortunately, I was impacted by the recent company-wide layoffs at [Company Name] and my role as [Title] has been eliminated."

### 2. The Highlight Reel

Use the next few lines to summarize what you achieved. Remind your network (and potential employers) of your value.

Pick two or three specific wins. Quantify them if possible. This is your chance to show that the layoff was a financial decision, not a performance issue.

### 3. The Gratitude Section

Thank your former team. Even if you are angry, expressing gratitude shows immense emotional intelligence. Recruiters look for candidates who handle adversity with grace.

Tag a few specific managers or mentors who helped you. This often prompts them to leave glowing recommendations in your comments, boosting your post's reach.

### 4. The Clear Call to Action

Tell people exactly what you are looking for next. If you are vague, you will get vague leads.

State your target roles, your preferred industry, and whether you want remote, hybrid, or on-site work. Finally, ask your network to engage with the post to help with the [LinkedIn algorithm](/blog/linkedin-algorithm-tips-increase-post-reach).

## 3 Copy-Paste Layoff Post Templates

If you are struggling to find the right words, use these templates as a starting point. [Draft your post](/blog/how-to-draft-linkedin-posts) carefully and customize the bracketed text.

### Template 1: The Direct & Professional Approach

> Like many others, I was impacted by the recent layoffs at [Company Name]. While this is a difficult transition, I am incredibly proud of what my team and I accomplished over the last [Time Period].
>
> During my time there, I:
>
> - [Achievement 1 with metric]
> - [Achievement 2 with metric]
> - [Achievement 3 with metric]
>
> A huge thank you to [Tag 1-2 people] for your leadership and to my entire team for the unforgettable experience.
>
> I am now actively looking for [Target Role 1] or [Target Role 2] roles in the [Industry] space (Remote or [City]).
>
> If you know of any open roles or would be open to a quick networking chat, I would love to connect. Any likes, comments, or shares are greatly appreciated! #OpenToWork #JobSearch

### Template 2: The Short & Sweet Approach

> After [Number] great years, my journey at [Company Name] has unexpectedly come to an end due to company-wide layoffs.
>
> I am walking away with incredible experience in [Skill 1], [Skill 2], and [Skill 3]. Thank you to everyone who made this chapter so special.
>
> I am officially #OpenToWork and looking for my next opportunity as a [Target Role]. Please let me know if you hear of any roles that might be a fit!

### Template 3: The Leadership Approach

> Today marks the end of an era. Due to restructuring at [Company Name], my role as [Title] has been eliminated.
>
> Building and leading the [Department/Team] has been the highlight of my career. I am so proud of our work to [Major Company Goal Achieved]. To my team: watching you grow has been an absolute privilege.
>
> As I figure out my next move, I am looking for [Target Leadership Role] opportunities where I can help scale [Specific Type of Operations].
>
> If your team is hiring, or if you just want to grab a virtual coffee, my inbox is open.

## Mistakes to Avoid

Before you hit publish, make sure you avoid these common traps.

**Do not vent.** No matter how poorly the company handled the situation, badmouthing them on LinkedIn makes you look like a liability to future employers. Keep it classy.

**Do not rush.** You do not need to post within five minutes of getting the news. Take a few days to process your emotions. A calm, collected post always performs better.

**Do not forget formatting.** Break up large blocks of text. Use bullet points for your achievements. Keep your [post length](/blog/linkedin-post-length) reasonable.

## Conclusion

Getting laid off is a major setback, but your LinkedIn network is your safety net. By keeping your announcement professional, highlighting your wins, and clearly stating what you want next, you set yourself up for a fast recovery.

Take your time, use a [LinkedIn posting strategy](/blog/linkedin-posting-best-practices), and lean on your connections.
