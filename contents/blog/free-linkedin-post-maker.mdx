## How to Use the LinkedIn Post Maker to Stand Out

Start by drafting your core message without worrying about formatting. Get your ideas down first. Once the raw content is ready, use the maker to refine it. 

Apply bold text to your main headings and essential metrics. Use bullet points to list actionable steps. If you struggle to generate ideas, you can even explore an [AI LinkedIn post generator](/blog/ai-linkedin-post-generator-guide) to get the initial draft started. 

Always check the preview before copying the text. Make sure your first three lines are compelling enough to stop the scroll. If the preview looks cluttered, add line breaks. 

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I make LinkedIn posts for free?

Yes, you can create and format your LinkedIn posts entirely for free using online tools. These platforms let you write, apply text styles, and preview your content without any subscription fees.

### Does formatting increase engagement?

Absolutely. Posts that are easy to read receive significantly more engagement. Using bold text, bullet points, and strategic whitespace keeps readers on your post longer, signaling to the algorithm that your content is valuable.

### Should I draft posts before publishing?

Yes. Drafting your content in a dedicated maker prevents accidental publishing and allows you to review your work with fresh eyes. It also makes it easier to build a consistent [LinkedIn content calendar](/blog/linkedin-content-calendar).
