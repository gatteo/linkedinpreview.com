## Can You Edit a LinkedIn Poll Question or Options?

**No. You cannot edit a LinkedIn poll once it has been published.**

LinkedIn strictly prohibits editing the poll question, the voting options, or the poll duration after it goes live. This rule exists to protect the integrity of the voting data. If you could change an option from "Yes" to "No" halfway through the week, it would invalidate all previous responses.

If you spot a typo in your poll question or answer choices, your only two options are to leave the mistake as it is or delete the post entirely and start over.

### What About the Text Above the Poll?

While you cannot touch the actual poll module, **you can edit the text caption** that accompanies your poll.

If you introduced your poll with a few paragraphs of text and spotted a spelling error in that introduction, you can safely hit the "Edit" button on the post to fix it. Just keep in mind that editing the caption will not [notify your connections](/blog/how-to-edit-linkedin-post-notifications), so they will not know you made a change.

## When Should You Delete and Repost a Poll?

Because you cannot edit a LinkedIn poll directly, you will sometimes have to make the hard choice to delete it. Here is when you should pull the plug versus when you should let it ride.

### 1. Delete If: The Core Meaning is Confusing

If you accidentally swapped two answers, wrote a confusing question, or forgot a crucial "Other" option, delete the poll immediately. Bad data is worse than no data. If you catch the error within the first 10 minutes, delete it, fix it, and republish.

### 2. Delete If: You Set the Wrong Duration

By default, LinkedIn sets polls to run for one week. If you meant to run a quick 24-hour poll and forgot to change the dropdown menu, you cannot adjust the timer later. You must delete and recreate the post.

### 3. Keep If: It is Just a Minor Typo

If you misspelled a word in an answer choice but the meaning is still 100% clear, leave it. If the poll already has dozens of votes, deleting it will frustrate the people who already took the time to engage with your content. You can always add a quick, lighthearted comment acknowledging your typo.

## How to Delete Your LinkedIn Poll

If the mistake is too big to ignore, deleting the poll is your only option.

**Step-by-step instructions to delete:**

1. Navigate to your LinkedIn feed or your profile's Activity section.
2. Find the poll you want to remove.
3. Click the **three dots (...)** in the top right corner of the post.
4. Select **Delete post** from the dropdown menu.
5. Confirm the deletion.

Once deleted, all votes and comments associated with that poll are gone forever. You cannot recover them.

## Best Practices to Avoid Poll Mistakes

Since you cannot edit a LinkedIn poll, prevention is your best strategy. Always follow a strict pre-publishing routine.

- **Draft externally:** Write your poll question and options in a separate document before pasting them into LinkedIn. This gives you a chance to spot errors.
- **Check character limits:** Poll questions are limited to 140 characters, and each option is limited to 30 characters. Keep your text tight.
- **Use a preview tool:** Run your introductory text through a formatting tool to ensure your [bold text](/blog/how-to-add-bold-text-to-linkedin-posts) and line breaks look professional on mobile devices.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I add a new option to an active poll?

No. Once the poll is published, you cannot add, remove, or rearrange any of the choices. You are locked into the options you originally set.

### Can I change my own vote on a LinkedIn poll?

No. Once you cast a vote on someone else's poll (or your own), you cannot undo or change your selection. Your vote is final.

### Can I see who voted on my poll?

Yes, but only if you are the creator of the poll. You can click on the vote count to see a list of who voted for which option. Other users can only see the overall percentages.

## Conclusion

The answer to whether you can edit a LinkedIn poll is a hard no. While you can update the text caption above it, the poll itself is locked the second you hit publish.

To avoid losing valuable engagement, always double-check your spelling, duration settings, and answer choices before sharing your poll with the world.
