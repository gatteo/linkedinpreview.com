Announcing you are looking for a new role can feel intimidating. But a well-crafted open to work LinkedIn post is one of the fastest ways to activate your network and land interviews.

When done right, an "open to work" announcement bypasses the resume pile. It puts your value directly in front of hiring managers, recruiters, and former colleagues who can refer you. Instead of just turning on the green banner, a dedicated post gives context and clarity to your job search.

## Elements of a High-Converting Job Search Post

The biggest mistake job seekers make is being too vague. "I'm looking for a job" doesn't give your network enough information to help you. A successful open to work LinkedIn post needs specific ingredients.

- **Clear target roles** - Name the exact job titles you want. Don't say "open to opportunities." Say "seeking Senior Product Marketing Manager roles."
- **Specific location or setup** - Mention if you want remote, hybrid, or on-site work in a specific city.
- **Your core value proposition** - Highlight 2-3 specific wins or skills. Give people a reason to hire you.
- **A direct call to action** - Tell people exactly how to help. Ask them to comment, tag recruiters, or share the post.

Structure your announcement using the [1-3-1 formatting framework](/blog/linkedin-post-format-1-3-1-structure) to ensure it is readable on mobile devices.

## Open to Work LinkedIn Post Examples

Here are three templates you can customize based on your situation.

### Template 1: The Direct and Professional Approach

Use this template if you want to keep things straightforward and focused purely on your skills and target roles.

> I am officially looking for my next opportunity!
>
> After a great run at [Company Name], I am currently seeking a new role as a [Target Title 1] or [Target Title 2].
>
> Over the last [Number] years, I have specialized in [Core Skill 1] and [Core Skill 2]. Most recently, I helped my team achieve [Specific Metric/Result, e.g., a 40% increase in Q3 revenue].
>
> Here is exactly what I am looking for:
>
> - Roles: [Target Title]
> - Location: [Remote / City]
> - Industry: [Target Industry]
>
> If you know of any open roles or teams hiring, I would love to connect. Please feel free to send me a DM or tag recruiters in the comments below. Likes and shares are also hugely appreciated to help expand my reach!

### Template 2: The Story-Driven Post

Stories perform incredibly well on LinkedIn. If you have been impacted by recent cuts, this format pairs well with a [LinkedIn layoff post](/blog/how-to-write-linkedin-layoff-post).

> Sometimes the unexpected happens, but it usually leads to the next great chapter.
>
> Due to recent restructuring at [Company Name], my role was eliminated. While it is hard to say goodbye to such a talented team, I am excited to see what is next.
>
> I am now actively looking for [Target Role] opportunities.
>
> My proudest moments from the last few years include:
>
> - Launching [Project Name] which drove [Result]
> - Scaling our [Department] from [X] to [Y]
> - Building a culture of [Core Value]
>
> I am looking for a [Remote/Hybrid] role in the [Industry] space where I can bring my expertise in [Core Skill].
>
> If your team is hiring, or if you know someone who is, let's chat. Thank you to everyone who has reached out so far - your support means everything.

### Template 3: The Career Pivot

If you are transitioning into a new industry or role, your post needs to connect the dots for the reader.

> I am making a pivot! After [Number] years in [Current Field], I am actively transitioning into [New Field/Role].
>
> My time in [Current Field] taught me how to [Transferable Skill 1] and [Transferable Skill 2] - skills that translate perfectly into [New Field].
>
> Over the past few months, I have been upskilling by [mention a course, certification, or personal project]. Now, I am ready to bring my unique perspective to a forward-thinking team.
>
> I am seeking [Target Title] roles in the [Target Industry] sector.
>
> If you have advice for someone entering this space, or if you know of any junior/mid-level openings, my DMs are open. Any introductions would be incredibly helpful!

## Best Practices for Formatting Your Post

Even the best copy will fail if it looks like a wall of text. LinkedIn truncates posts after the first few lines, so your hook needs to be strong.

Use plenty of white space. Break up long paragraphs into single sentences. When listing your skills or target roles, use bullet points instead of commas. If you want to emphasize specific metrics or job titles, use bold text to draw the eye.

Always include a clear [call to action](/blog/linkedin-call-to-action-examples) at the end. Ask your network to engage. A simple "please like or comment to help this reach more feeds" goes a long way.

## FAQ

### Should I use the green Open to Work banner?

Yes. The green #OpenToWork banner makes it immediately obvious to recruiters scrolling through the feed that you are available. It increases your visibility in recruiter searches.

### What hashtags should I use for a job search post?

Stick to 3-5 relevant hashtags at the bottom of your post. Good options include #OpenToWork, #JobSearch, #[YourIndustry], and #[YourTargetRole].

### When is the best time to post an open to work announcement?

Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday mornings between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM local time tend to get the highest visibility from professionals and hiring managers.

## Conclusion

An open to work LinkedIn post is a powerful networking tool. By clearly stating what you want, highlighting your value, and formatting the text for readability, you make it easy for your network to help you land your next role.
