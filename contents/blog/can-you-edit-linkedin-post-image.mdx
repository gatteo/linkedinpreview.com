You just hit the "Post" button on a brilliant LinkedIn update. But a second later, you realize you attached the wrong image. Or worse, you forgot to add the photo entirely.

Panic sets in. You click the three dots in the corner of your post, select "Edit post," and try to fix it. But the image options are nowhere to be found.

If you are wondering how to edit a LinkedIn post image after it is already live, you are not alone. Thousands of professionals search for a way to add or replace photos on their published content every month.

Here is exactly how LinkedIn handles post media editing and the workaround you need to use.

## The Short Answer: No, You Cannot Edit Images

Unfortunately, **you cannot edit, add, or remove an image on a LinkedIn post after it has been published.**

Once a post goes live on the LinkedIn feed, its media attachments become permanent. This rule applies to all forms of rich media, including:

- Single images and photos
- Multiple image galleries
- Videos
- Document PDFs and carousels
- Polls

If you try to edit the post natively, you will see that the text box is editable, but the image itself is locked in place. There is no hidden button or premium feature that bypasses this restriction.

## What Can You Edit on a Published Post?

While your images are locked, LinkedIn does give you the flexibility to change the written portion of your update.

When you click "Edit post" on a published update, you can safely modify:

- **The text and copy:** You can fix typos, rewrite sentences, or add entirely new paragraphs.
- **Mentions and tags:** You can tag new people or companies, or remove tags that were broken.
- **Hashtags:** You can add or remove hashtags at the bottom of your post.
- **External links:** You can add URLs into the text body.

Keep in mind that if your post is performing well, making significant edits to the text can temporarily stall its reach while the LinkedIn algorithm re-evaluates the new content.

## How to Fix a Wrong Image on LinkedIn

Since you cannot swap out the image directly, there is only one way to fix the mistake: **you must delete the original post and publish a new one.**

If you catch the error immediately, this is a minor inconvenience. Follow these exact steps to ensure you do not lose your carefully crafted text:

1. **Copy your text first:** Before you do anything, highlight the text of your published post and copy it to your clipboard. If you used special formatting, paste it into a safe notes app.
2. **Delete the post:** Click the three dots (More icon) in the top right corner of the wrong post and select "Delete post."
3. **Start a new post:** Open a fresh post creation window on LinkedIn.
4. **Attach the correct image:** Upload the right photo or gallery first.
5. **Paste your text:** Paste your copied text back into the caption area.
6. **Publish:** Review everything carefully, then hit publish.

## Will Deleting and Reposting Hurt My LinkedIn Reach?

Many creators worry that deleting a post and immediately republishing it will trigger a spam filter or hurt their engagement.

If you delete and repost within the first few minutes of publishing, the negative impact is essentially zero. The algorithm will treat your new upload as a standard piece of content.

However, if your post has been live for several hours and has already accumulated likes and comments, deleting it means you lose all of that engagement. The social proof is gone, and the new post will have to start from scratch.

In this scenario, you have to weigh the options. If the image error is minor (like a small crop issue), it is usually better to leave the post alone and keep the engagement. If the image is completely wrong, off-brand, or contains sensitive information, you must delete it regardless of the lost likes.

## How to Avoid Image Mistakes Before Publishing

The best way to handle LinkedIn image formatting issues is to prevent them from happening in the first place.

The native LinkedIn composer is notoriously clunky. It often crops images strangely on mobile devices or fails to show you exactly how your text will align with your photo.

To ensure your posts look perfect every time, use a dedicated preview tool before you publish. By pasting your text and uploading your image into a simulator, you can catch typos, test your hooks, and verify that your photos are formatted perfectly for both desktop and mobile feeds.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Can I add a photo to a LinkedIn post after posting?

No. You cannot add a new photo to a text-only post once it has been published. You must delete the post and create a new one with the image attached.

### Can I edit a link preview image on LinkedIn?

If you share an external URL and the link preview thumbnail is wrong, you cannot change it directly in the post. You must update the Open Graph image on your website and use the official LinkedIn Post Inspector to scrape the new image.

### Does LinkedIn notify people if I delete and repost?

No, LinkedIn does not send notifications when you delete a post. However, if you tagged people in the original post, they may receive a new notification when you tag them again in the reposted version.

### Can you rearrange multiple photos after publishing?

No. If you upload a gallery of images and they are in the wrong order, you cannot rearrange them after the post goes live. You will need to delete the post and re-upload the images in the correct sequence.
