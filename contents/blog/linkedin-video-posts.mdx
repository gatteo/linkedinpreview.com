Video content on LinkedIn is no longer optional - it's one of the most effective formats for reaching your professional network in 2026.

LinkedIn video posts grew by **53% year-over-year**, and the platform's algorithm now actively prioritizes short-form video content in feeds. If you're still posting only text and images, you're missing out on massive engagement opportunities.

This guide covers everything you need to know about LinkedIn video posts: how they work, the technical specs you need to follow, step-by-step posting instructions, and proven best practices for video captions and content strategy.

## Why LinkedIn Video Posts Work in 2026

LinkedIn video content consistently outperforms other post formats. Here's why video is dominating the platform:

**Algorithm Prioritization**  
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm actively boosts video content in feeds. The platform reports that **video posts receive 5x more engagement than static posts**. Short-form vertical videos get even more algorithmic favor on mobile devices.

**Higher Engagement Rates**  
Video posts generate significantly more reactions, comments, and shares compared to text-only posts. Users spend more time watching videos (increased dwell time), which signals to the algorithm that your content is valuable.

**Mobile-First Consumption**  
Over 60% of LinkedIn traffic comes from mobile devices. Vertical video formats (4:5 and 9:16 aspect ratios) occupy more screen real estate and perform better than landscape videos on mobile.

**Professional Authenticity**  
Video allows you to showcase your personality, expertise, and authenticity in ways that text cannot. Face-to-camera content builds trust faster and creates stronger professional connections.

**Better Reach**  
LinkedIn Live videos receive **7x more reactions and 24x more comments** compared to regular video posts. The platform notifies your network when you go live, creating immediate engagement spikes.

## Types of LinkedIn Video Posts

LinkedIn supports several video formats. Here's what you need to know about each type:

### Native Feed Videos

These are standard video posts you upload directly to your LinkedIn feed. They autoplay as users scroll, making them highly visible.

- **Best for:** Thought leadership, quick tips, behind-the-scenes content, personal stories
- **Length:** 3 seconds to 10 minutes
- **Format:** MP4, MOV, AVI, WEBM, MKV, WMM, and others
- **Max file size:** 5GB

### Short-Form Videos

LinkedIn's answer to TikTok and Instagram Reels. These vertical videos appear in LinkedIn's dedicated short-form feed.

- **Best for:** Quick tips, hooks, trending topics, mobile-first content
- **Length:** Up to 2 minutes (under 15 seconds performs best)
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 vertical or 4:5
- **Engagement:** 58% higher engagement on mobile vs landscape videos

### LinkedIn Live

Real-time video broadcasts to your network or Company Page. Requires pre-approval from LinkedIn (account must be 30+ days old with established activity).

- **Best for:** Webinars, Q&As, product launches, panel discussions
- **Length:** 10 minutes to 4 hours (30-60 minutes recommended)
- **Notification:** Followers receive alerts when you start streaming
- **Replay value:** Live videos remain viewable after the broadcast ends

### LinkedIn Video Ads

Paid sponsored video content that appears in user feeds with advanced targeting options.

- **Best for:** Product demos, brand awareness campaigns, lead generation
- **Length:** 3 seconds to 30 minutes (15-30 seconds recommended for placement)
- **Max file size:** 500MB
- **Format:** MP4 only for ads

For most professionals and businesses, **native feed videos** and **short-form videos** offer the best ROI without requiring paid promotion.

## How to Post a Video on LinkedIn

### Desktop Instructions

1. **Navigate to LinkedIn.com** and log into your account
2. **Click "Start a post"** at the top of your feed
3. **Select the video icon** (camera with a circle) from the options
4. **Choose your video file** from your computer
5. **Wait for upload** - LinkedIn processes the video (may take 1-2 minutes)
6. **Write your caption** - Add your text, hashtags, and mentions
7. **Optional: Add a cover image** - Select a thumbnail frame
8. **Click "Post"** to publish

### Mobile Instructions (iOS/Android)

1. **Open the LinkedIn app** and tap the "+" icon at the bottom
2. **Select "Video"** from the post options
3. **Choose "Record" or "Upload"**:
   - **Record:** Use your phone camera to film directly
   - **Upload:** Select an existing video from your gallery
4. **Trim your video** if needed using the editing tools
5. **Add captions/text overlays** (optional, via LinkedIn's built-in editor)
6. **Write your post caption** in the text field
7. **Tap "Post"** to publish

**Pro tip:** Film vertical videos (hold your phone upright) for better mobile engagement. Vertical videos receive 58% more engagement than landscape videos on mobile devices.

## LinkedIn Video Specs & Requirements

Getting your video specs right ensures smooth uploads and optimal playback. Here's what LinkedIn accepts:

### Video File Requirements

**Accepted formats:**
- MP4 (recommended)
- MOV
- AVI
- WEBM
- MKV
- WMV
- MPEG formats

**File size:**
- Native videos: 75KB to 5GB
- Company Page videos: 75KB to 200MB
- Video ads: 75KB to 500MB

### Video Length

- **Minimum:** 3 seconds
- **Maximum:** 10 minutes (native), 2 minutes (short-form), 30 minutes (ads)
- **Optimal length:** 30 seconds to 2 minutes for maximum engagement

Videos under 90 seconds perform best for engagement. Longer videos (5-10 minutes) work better for educational content or in-depth tutorials.

### Aspect Ratios

**Recommended ratios:**
- **16:9 landscape** - Traditional horizontal video (good for desktop)
- **1:1 square** - Works on both mobile and desktop
- **4:5 vertical** - Best for mobile feeds (occupies more screen)
- **9:16 vertical** - Ideal for short-form content (full-screen mobile)

**Vertical videos (4:5 or 9:16) get significantly more engagement on mobile** devices where most LinkedIn browsing happens.

### Resolution

- **Minimum:** 256×154 pixels
- **Maximum:** 4096×2304 pixels
- **Recommended:** 1080p (1920×1080 for landscape, 1080×1920 for vertical)

Higher resolution = more professional appearance, but file size increases accordingly.

### Frame Rate

- **Maximum:** 60 fps
- **Recommended:** 30 fps (standard for most content)

### Audio

- **Optional** for most video types
- **Required** for LinkedIn Live
- **Format:** AAC or MPEG4 audio codec

**Important:** Many users watch videos without sound. Always add captions or text overlays to convey your message visually.

## Best Practices for Video Post Captions

The text caption you write for your video post matters just as much as the video itself. Here's how to write captions that drive engagement:

### Start With a Strong Hook

Your first 1-2 lines appear before users click "see more." Make them count.

**Good hooks:**
- "I spent $50,000 learning this lesson the hard way..."
- "3 mistakes that kill your LinkedIn video engagement (and how to fix them)"
- "Why your videos aren't getting views (it's not the algorithm)"

### Format for Mobile

Use short paragraphs (2-3 lines max), line breaks, and spacing. Wall-of-text captions get skipped.

**Example:**
```
This is how I 10x my LinkedIn video reach in 30 days.

(Without posting more often)

The secret? It's not about video quality.

It's about timing + captions + hook strategy.

Here's the exact framework I used:
```

### Include Keywords and Hashtags

LinkedIn's search and recommendation algorithm scans caption text. Include relevant keywords naturally.

- Use 3-5 targeted hashtags (e.g., #LinkedInTips #VideoMarketing #ContentStrategy)
- Don't stuff keywords unnaturally
- Place hashtags at the end of your caption

### Add a Clear Call-to-Action

Tell viewers exactly what you want them to do next.

**Engagement CTAs:**
- "What's your biggest video challenge? Comment below."
- "Save this for later if you're planning to post more videos."
- "Repost if this helped you."

**Traffic CTAs:**
- "Link to the full guide in the comments."
- "DM me 'VIDEO' for the complete checklist."

### Tag Relevant People and Companies

Tagging increases reach and encourages shares. Tag:
- People featured in the video
- Collaborators or guests
- Brands or companies mentioned
- Industry thought leaders (when relevant)

### Preview Your Caption Before Publishing

Use a LinkedIn post preview tool to see exactly how your caption will look before publishing. Check:
- Hook visibility (first 2 lines)
- Line breaks and spacing
- Mobile vs desktop appearance
- Character count (aim for 1300-2000 characters for optimal engagement)

## Tips for High-Performing Video Posts

Beyond specs and captions, these tactics will help your video posts perform better:

### 1. Use Captions/Subtitles

**85% of LinkedIn videos are watched without sound.** Add captions to ensure your message gets across.

- Use LinkedIn's auto-caption feature (available for most videos)
- Or add captions during video editing before upload
- Captions increase engagement by making videos accessible to more viewers

### 2. Post at Optimal Times

Video posts perform best when your target audience is active.

**Best times to post videos:**
- **Tuesday-Friday, 8-11 AM** (in your audience's time zone)
- Avoid early Monday mornings (low engagement)
- Avoid weekends unless your audience is B2C

### 3. Use a Strong Thumbnail

LinkedIn auto-generates thumbnails from your video, but you can select a custom frame.

**Choose a thumbnail that:**
- Shows your face (if it's a face-to-camera video)
- Has clear text overlays (readable at small sizes)
- Creates curiosity or signals value

### 4. Front-Load Your Value

Deliver value in the first 3-5 seconds. Don't waste time with long intros.

**Bad opening:** "Hey everyone, welcome back to my LinkedIn, today I want to talk about..."

**Good opening:** "Here's the 3-step framework that 10x my video reach..."

### 5. Ask a Question in the First 10 Seconds

Videos that ask a direct question within the first 10 seconds have a **19% higher comment rate**.

Example: "What's the biggest mistake you're making with LinkedIn videos? Comment below after watching."

### 6. Post Consistently

Consistency matters more than perfection. Aim for:
- **1-2 video posts per week** minimum
- Post on the same days/times when possible
- Build momentum over time (algorithm rewards consistency)

### 7. Repurpose Content

Don't create video content only for LinkedIn. Repurpose across platforms:
- Record one longer video, cut into multiple short clips
- Repost top-performing videos from other platforms
- Turn blog posts into quick video summaries

### 8. Reply to Comments Quickly

Respond to comments within the first hour of posting. Early engagement signals quality to the algorithm and encourages more interaction.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

These mistakes hurt your video post performance:

### 1. Wrong Video Specs

Uploading videos that don't meet LinkedIn's requirements causes:
- Failed uploads
- Poor video quality after processing
- Cropped or distorted playback

**Solution:** Always check file size, format, and resolution before uploading.

### 2. No Hook or Caption

Posting a video with no caption or a weak hook wastes the opportunity to provide context and drive engagement.

**Solution:** Write a compelling caption that explains what the video is about and why viewers should watch.

### 3. Landscape Videos on Mobile

Horizontal videos occupy less screen space on mobile feeds and get scrolled past more often.

**Solution:** Film or edit videos in vertical format (9:16 or 4:5 aspect ratio) for mobile-first engagement.

### 4. No Call-to-Action

If you don't tell viewers what to do next, they won't do anything. No CTA = low engagement.

**Solution:** End every video post with a clear CTA (comment, share, save, visit link in comments, DM).

### 5. Ignoring Comments

Failing to respond to comments signals low engagement to the algorithm and discourages future interaction.

**Solution:** Reply to every comment within the first 1-2 hours of posting.

### 6. Posting External Video Links

Pasting a YouTube or Vimeo link instead of uploading directly to LinkedIn dramatically reduces reach. The algorithm deprioritizes external links.

**Solution:** Always upload videos natively to LinkedIn. Save external links for the comments section.

### 7. Using All 3000 Characters

Super long captions get truncated behind "see more." Most users won't expand them.

**Solution:** Keep captions between 1300-2000 characters. Front-load key points in the first 150 characters (before the "see more" fold).

### 8. No Video Consistency

Posting one video every few months won't build momentum or audience.

**Solution:** Commit to a posting schedule (even if it's just one video every week). Consistency compounds over time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best video length for LinkedIn?

**30 seconds to 2 minutes** works best for most content types. Videos under 90 seconds have the highest completion rates and engagement. Longer videos (5-10 minutes) work for in-depth educational content but should be split into shorter clips when possible.

### What video format does LinkedIn prefer?

**MP4 is the most universally supported format** across all LinkedIn video types. Use 1080p resolution with a 16:9 aspect ratio for landscape or 9:16 for vertical content. Export at 30 fps for optimal quality and compatibility.

### Do I need captions on LinkedIn videos?

Captions are optional but **highly recommended**. 85% of LinkedIn videos are watched without sound, so captions ensure your message reaches viewers even when audio is off. Videos with captions also perform better in accessibility and engagement.

### Can I post the same video on LinkedIn and other platforms?

Yes, you can (and should) repurpose video content across platforms. However, **always upload videos natively to LinkedIn** rather than sharing links to YouTube or other platforms. Native videos get significantly more reach and engagement.

### What's the difference between native LinkedIn videos and video ads?

**Native videos** are organic posts you upload directly to your feed or Company Page (free). **Video ads** are paid sponsored content with advanced targeting options, stricter format requirements (MP4 only), and higher production quality standards. For most users, native videos offer better ROI.

### How do I get more views on LinkedIn videos?

To increase video views:
1. **Use vertical video formats** (9:16 or 4:5) for mobile
2. **Write strong captions with hooks** in the first 2 lines
3. **Post during peak times** (Tuesday-Friday, 8-11 AM)
4. **Add captions/subtitles** to your videos
5. **Reply to comments quickly** (within first hour)
6. **Ask questions** to drive engagement
7. **Post consistently** (1-2 videos per week minimum)
