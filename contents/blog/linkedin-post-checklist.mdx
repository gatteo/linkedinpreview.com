## The 10-Step LinkedIn Post Checklist

### 1. Does your hook stand out?
The first two lines determine if users click "See more." Ensure your hook is punchy, provides a clear benefit, and isn't cut off awkwardly on mobile.

*   **Hook Checklist**: Does it state a clear, actionable benefit? Does it intrigue? Is the formatting clean?

### 2. Is the "See more" line optimized?
Check where your post truncates. If your hook stops in the middle of a sentence, adjust your wording or spacing so the truncation happens at a natural pause.

*   **Pro Tip**: Aim for 100-140 characters before the "See more" link appears.

### 3. Is your formatting scannable?
Break up dense paragraphs with line breaks. Use bold text for key takeaways and bullet points for lists.

*   **Why**: Scannability reduces cognitive load. If it takes longer than 2 seconds to understand the point, you've lost the reader.

### 4. Are your images or media properly cropped?
If you are uploading a single image or carousel, preview it to make sure important text isn't cropped out on mobile.

*   **Quick Test**: Upload the image to the preview tool to verify it doesn't lose context.

### 5. Did you include a clear CTA?
Every post needs an action. Whether it is asking a question, directing readers to your profile, or suggesting a click, ensure your CTA is direct and easy to follow.

*   **Avoid ambiguity**: "Let me know your thoughts" is okay, but "What is your biggest struggle with [Topic]?" is better.

### 6. Are your links in the right place?
LinkedIn discourages external links in the post body, which can hurt reach. Place your link in the first comment or your profile's Featured section instead.

*   **Why**: It keeps readers on the platform for longer, which the algorithm rewards.

### 7. Did you check your tone and grammar?
A quick scan for clarity and professional tone is essential. Emojis can add personality, but ensure they are used sparingly and for a clear purpose.

*   **Self-Correction**: Read your post out loud. Does it sound conversational and natural?

### 8. Is the tone professional but authentic?
LinkedIn rewards authenticity. Ensure your post sounds like *you*, not a corporate brochure or an over-polished press release.

*   **Key**: Share personal insights, wins, or hard-won lessons.

### 9. Did you preview on mobile?
60%+ of your audience is on mobile. If your formatting or image layout looks bad on a phone, it looks bad to the majority of your audience.

*   **Why**: Mobile users are the most likely to drop off.

### 10. Did you check your hashtags?
Use 3-5 relevant, high-impact hashtags. Avoid "hashtag stuffing" (using 10+), which the algorithm now penalizes as spam.

*   **Placement**: Put hashtags at the bottom, after your CTA and any secondary information.

## Why Pre-Publishing Checklists Work

Writing great content is only half the battle. The other half is ensuring that your content *actually reaches* your audience and is *easy* for them to consume. 

Most creators struggle with consistency and performance because they lack a repeatable process. By making this 10-step checklist a mandatory part of your workflow, you achieve three things:

1.  **Reduce Errors**: You eliminate small, avoidable mistakes that undermine your professional image.
2.  **Optimize for the Algorithm**: You ensure your post structure aligns with what the algorithm currently favors (like scannability and high-value hooks).
3.  **Build Confidence**: You approach every post knowing that it meets your quality standards, taking the anxiety out of hitting "publish."

## Iterating on Your Results

Once your post is live, your job isn't done. Use your post analytics to see how your checklist improved your results.

- **Check Impressions**: Did your hook effectively reduce the "See more" drop-off?
- **Analyze Engagement**: Did the bolding and bullet points increase the time users spent on the post?
- **Review Profile Views**: Did the CTA successfully drive traffic from the post to your profile?

Use these insights to refine your next post.
