Your LinkedIn profile is more than just a resume - it is a dynamic portfolio. If you aren't using the Featured section, you are leaving your best professional work hidden.

The Featured section sits right at the top of your profile, serving as a high-visibility "billboard" for the content you want visitors to see first. Whether you want to drive traffic to your website, showcase your top-performing posts, or highlight a recent certification, this is the place to do it.

In 2026, with LinkedIn’s algorithm prioritizing depth of engagement, using your Featured section to direct traffic away from the feed and toward your high-value assets is a proven strategy to boost your authority.

## Why the Featured Section Matters

1.  **High Visibility**: It sits above the fold on mobile and desktop, ensuring high exposure.
2.  **Authority Building**: It provides instant proof of your expertise through tangible work samples.
3.  **Traffic Driver**: It is the most effective way to funnel profile visitors to your personal website, newsletter, or specific landing page.
4.  **Content Lifecycle**: While LinkedIn feed posts disappear quickly, your Featured section keeps your best, most evergreen content accessible indefinitely.

## What Should You Feature?

The key is variety. Don't just fill it with one type of content.

- **High-Engagement Posts**: Highlight your best-performing content that generated comments, shares, or saves.
- **Relevant Links**: Direct traffic to your website, newsletter, or a specific lead-gen landing page.
- **Media/Portfolios**: PDF case studies, images of your design work, or videos of your presentations.
- **Articles**: If you write LinkedIn Articles, showcase the ones that demonstrate deep industry expertise.
- **Professional Certifications**: Highlight recent milestones that validate your skills.

## Best Practices for Optimization

### 1. Lead with Your Strongest Asset
LinkedIn displays featured items in a horizontally scrollable carousel. The first item gets the most attention, so make it your most impactful asset (e.g., your primary website link or a powerful case study).

### 2. Update Regularly
The Featured section is not a static tombstone. Update it based on your current goals - if you are hiring, feature your careers page. If you are launching a product, feature the demo link.

### 3. Use Clear Titles and Descriptions
Don't just upload a file and leave the title empty. Use clear, descriptive titles that explain *why* the visitor should click.

### 4. Optimize the Link Preview
When adding a link, ensure your website has a custom, optimized OpenGraph image (OG image) so the thumbnail looks professional and eye-catching.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Letting it get outdated**: Having a "2023 Webinar" link in 2026 signals a lack of active engagement.
- **Cluttering it**: Featuring 10 different items makes it difficult for a user to know what to focus on. Keep it focused to 3-5 high-value assets.
- **Ignoring the mobile view**: Always check how your Featured section carousel appears on a phone. The first two items are the only ones immediately visible on small screens.

## Conclusion

The LinkedIn Featured section is your chance to curate the impression you make on visitors. By strategically choosing what to showcase and updating it regularly, you turn your profile into a powerful engine for building authority and driving traffic.
