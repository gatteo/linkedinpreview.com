## Why LinkedIn Line Breaks Disappear

LinkedIn's native text editor treats whitespace differently depending on where the text originated. When you write directly inside the LinkedIn app, hitting "Enter" usually creates a normal paragraph break.

The problem starts when you draft elsewhere. If a line consists of completely empty space, LinkedIn's rendering engine often ignores it and collapses the paragraphs. This is why a beautifully spaced draft in your notes app becomes a cluttered mess on the feed.

Viewers on mobile devices (which account for the majority of LinkedIn traffic) will immediately scroll past a giant block of text. To fix this, you need to trick the platform into thinking those empty lines actually contain content.

## How to Fix LinkedIn Paragraph Spacing

There are a few reliable methods to ensure your line breaks stay exactly where you put them.

### 1. Use the Invisible Character Trick

This is the most dependable method to maintain blank lines on LinkedIn. You insert an invisible Unicode character (like a zero-width space) on the empty line.

Since the line technically contains a character, LinkedIn will not collapse it.

- **Find an invisible character** - You can search online for "invisible character copy paste".
- **Paste it on empty lines** - Add it between your paragraphs where you want a blank line to remain.
- **Publish your post** - Your spacing will remain perfectly intact.

If you prefer not to use external tools, you can draft your content directly in the LinkedIn native editor.

While this avoids the copy-paste formatting bug, it is risky. The native editor lacks autosave features, meaning an accidental browser refresh can delete your entire draft. If you choose this route, always save a backup of your text first.

## Best Practices for LinkedIn Post Formatting

Adding line breaks is just the first step. To maximize engagement, you need to structure your post for readability.

- **Use short paragraphs** - Limit your paragraphs to one or two sentences. White space is your friend.
- **Hook your audience early** - The first few lines are crucial. Use a strong opening to encourage people to click the "See more" button. You can test yours with a [LinkedIn hook preview](/blog/linkedin-hook-preview).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does my LinkedIn About section lose its line breaks?

The About section on your profile is notorious for removing line breaks, particularly on the mobile app. The invisible character trick works here too. Paste an invisible character on every blank line you want to preserve.

### Can I just use a period on the empty line?

Yes, placing a single period (.) on an empty line will stop LinkedIn from collapsing it. However, this looks messy and unprofessional compared to a clean, empty space.

### Does paragraph spacing affect the LinkedIn algorithm?

Not directly, but it impacts readability. If your post is a giant block of text, users will scroll past. Lower engagement signals the algorithm to stop showing your post in the feed.
