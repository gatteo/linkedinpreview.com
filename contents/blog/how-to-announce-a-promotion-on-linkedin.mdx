Announcing a promotion on LinkedIn is the perfect way to celebrate your success and build professional visibility. But finding the right words can be tricky. You want to share the good news without sounding like you are bragging.

A great promotion post balances pride with gratitude. It acknowledges the hard work that got you there, thanks the people who supported you, and sets the stage for your next career chapter.

## Why You Should Announce Your Promotion on LinkedIn

Updating your profile is important, but a dedicated post about your promotion does something a simple profile update cannot: it tells your story.

A well-crafted promotion announcement:

- **Builds your personal brand:** It shows your network that you are growing, taking on new responsibilities, and delivering value.
- **Recognizes your mentors:** It gives you a public forum to thank the managers and colleagues who helped you grow.
- **Invites engagement:** Career updates are among the highest-engaging content types on the platform, putting you on the radar of recruiters and industry peers.

If you are wondering when to share the news, check our guide on the [best times to post on LinkedIn](/blog/best-times-to-post-on-linkedin) to maximize your visibility.

## 5 Steps to Craft the Perfect LinkedIn Promotion Post

The best promotion posts follow a specific structure. They hook the reader, tell a brief story, and focus heavily on the people who made the success possible.

### 1. Hook the Reader Immediately

Start with a strong opening line. Avoid the standard "I am thrilled to announce..." because everyone uses it. Instead, start with a reflection, a milestone, or a surprising fact about your journey. Want more ideas? Check out our list of [LinkedIn hook examples](/blog/linkedin-hook-examples).

### 2. Share the Story

Don't just state your new title. Share a brief story about what you learned in your previous role or a challenge you overcame to reach this point.

### 3. Express Gratitude

This is the most critical step. Tag your manager, your team, and any mentors who supported you. Highlighting others shows humility and leadership.

### 4. Look to the Future

Briefly mention what you are excited to tackle in your new role. This positions you as forward-thinking and ready for the challenge.

### 5. Format for Readability

Nobody wants to read a giant wall of text. Use short paragraphs and [bold text](/blog/how-to-add-bold-text-to-linkedin-posts) to highlight key takeaways. Bullet points work great for listing the people you want to thank or the goals you want to achieve.

## 3 LinkedIn Promotion Post Templates

Not sure where to start? Use these templates as a foundation and customize them to fit your voice.

### Template 1: The Grateful & Humble Approach

**Best for:** Standard corporate promotions and team-focused environments.

"Two years ago, I joined [Company Name] as a [Previous Title] with a goal to [mention a specific goal]. Today, I am excited to share that I have been promoted to [New Title]!

Looking back, the highlight of this journey has not just been the projects, but the people. A massive thank you to [Tag Manager] for your continuous guidance, and to [Tag Colleague 1] and [Tag Colleague 2] for being the best team I could ask for.

In this new role, I will be focusing on [mention 1-2 new responsibilities]. I cannot wait to get started and see what we build next."

### Template 2: The Story-Driven Journey

**Best for:** Major career leaps, overcoming obstacles, or non-linear career paths. (Pairs well with a [storytelling framework](/blog/linkedin-storytelling-framework)).

"If you told me [Number] years ago that I would be leading the [Department Name] team at [Company Name], I would not have believed you.

When I first started here, I struggled with [mention a challenge]. But thanks to incredible mentorship from [Tag Mentor] and a culture that encourages taking risks, I found my footing.

I am incredibly proud to announce my promotion to [New Title].

Thank you to everyone who answered my endless questions and pushed me to grow. The work is just getting started, and I am ready for the next chapter."

### Template 3: The Short & Sweet Update

**Best for:** Quick updates where you prefer to keep things concise and professional.

"I am happy to share that I am starting a new position as [New Title] at [Company Name]!

Thank you to [Tag Manager/Mentor] for the support and trust over the past [Time Period]. Excited to take on this new challenge and continue growing with such an amazing team."

## How to Add Your Promotion to Your LinkedIn Profile

Before you publish your post, make sure your actual profile is updated.

1. Go to your LinkedIn profile.
2. Scroll to the **Experience** section and click the "+" icon.
3. Select **Add position**.
4. Fill in your new title, start date, and details.
5. Under "Notify network", decide if you want LinkedIn to send an automated alert. If you are writing a custom post using the templates above, you may want to turn this off to avoid spamming your connections with two updates.

## Conclusion

Announcing a promotion on LinkedIn should feel exciting, not stressful. By focusing on gratitude, sharing a bit of your story, and formatting your text cleanly, you can craft a post that celebrates your win and engages your network.
