If you write content on LinkedIn, your entire success hinges on one specific element: the "See more" button. If people do not click it, the algorithm assumes your post is boring, and your reach instantly plummets.

Understanding exactly where the **LinkedIn "see more" cutoff** happens is the key to writing a hook that drives engagement. The problem is that LinkedIn truncates your text differently depending on the device your audience is using.

Here is exactly how the cutoff works in 2026 and how to format your hook to ensure people keep reading.

## Where Does the LinkedIn "See More" Cutoff Happen?

LinkedIn does not use a strict character limit to determine where to place the "See more" button. Instead, it relies on a combination of visible lines and character count.

### The Desktop Cutoff

On desktop, the LinkedIn "see more" cutoff typically happens at the end of the **third line**. If you do not use line breaks, this usually equals around 140 to 210 characters.

If you use spacing and formatting to separate your sentences, LinkedIn will hide everything after the third visible line of text (including blank lines).

### The Mobile Cutoff

On mobile devices (iOS and Android), the cutoff is slightly more aggressive due to narrower screen widths. The "see more" button generally appears after the **third line** as well, but because the screen is smaller, sentences wrap much faster.

A sentence that takes up one line on a desktop monitor might take up two or three lines on a smartphone, pushing your crucial hook below the fold.

## Why the "See More" Button Matters for the Algorithm

LinkedIn tracks "dwell time" (how long someone looks at your post) and active engagement. Clicking the "See more" button is a massive positive signal to the LinkedIn algorithm.

- **Signals intent:** It tells LinkedIn the reader is genuinely interested in your topic.
- **Increases dwell time:** Expanding the post keeps the user on your content longer.
- **Triggers distribution:** High click rates in the first hour of posting tell the algorithm to show your content to a wider audience outside your immediate network.

## 3 Ways to Format Your Hook for Maximum Clicks

You can control exactly how your hook appears by using strategic formatting.

### 1. The One-Line Statement

Start with a punchy, single-sentence statement that creates an immediate knowledge gap. Follow it with a blank line. This ensures your entire premise is visible on both mobile and desktop.

### 2. The List Teaser

People love lists. Start by numbering your points immediately, but format them so that point number two or three gets cut off mid-sentence. The desire to see the rest of the list forces the reader to expand the post.

### 3. The Forced Cutoff

You can artificially force the "See more" button higher up in your post by intentionally inserting line breaks. If you type a short sentence, hit Enter twice, and type another short sentence, the blank lines count toward the visible limit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does the "See more" cutoff change if I include an image?

The presence of an image or video does not significantly change the text cutoff rules. The text above the media will still truncate around the third line. However, media takes up more screen real estate, making a concise hook even more important.

### Can I change the cutoff point after publishing?

Yes, you can edit your post after it goes live to adjust the spacing. If you notice your hook is getting cut off awkwardly, you can edit the text and add or remove line breaks.

## Conclusion

Mastering the LinkedIn "see more" cutoff is one of the highest-ROI skills for content creators. Keep your hooks concise, use line breaks strategically, and always check how your text wraps on mobile screens.
